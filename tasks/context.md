## Research Knowledge Base

### Requirements Summary
- Research adversarial technologies affecting autonomous projectile guidance systems
- Focus on 5 key areas: Electronic warfare, Cybersecurity, Environmental hazards, Physical countermeasures, Regulatory constraints
- Non-military applications: Atmospheric research, materials testing, autonomous systems R&D
- Create comprehensive analysis covering: specific disruptive technologies, mitigation strategies, risk assessment, safety protocols, detection methods
- Include current state-of-the-art countermeasures and best practices

### Key Discoveries by Domain

#### Electronic Warfare Threats
- **GPS Jamming & Spoofing**: Primary battlefield threats identified in 2025 conflicts
- **HIMARS/Artillery Protection**: Systems being retrofitted with GPS protections based on Ukraine war experience
- **Advanced Navigation EP Systems**: Electronic Protection range for defense-ready INS systems emerging
- **FAA GNSS Interference Guide**: Version 1.0 released December 2025
- **ECCM Critical**: Electronic Counter-Countermeasures becoming essential for autonomous navigation
- **Solid-State Gyroscopes**: Potentially rendering traditional GPS jamming obsolete
- **LiDAR Vulnerabilities**: Perception systems vulnerable to physical adversarial attacks (arXiv, Sep 2024)

#### Cybersecurity Threats
- **Data Poisoning**: Significant emerging threat to Intelligent Transportation Systems
- **Partial-Information Attacks**: Targeting autonomous vehicle sensing via firmware/driver malware
- **AI Security Expansion**: Agentic AI solutions becoming targets in 2025
- **Longitudinal Cyber Attacks**: Lack situational awareness but disrupt safety-critical sensing
- **Internet Balkanization**: Affecting autonomous systems in 2025 threat landscape
- **Vision System Vulnerabilities**: Adversarial attacks across full stack and life-cycle

#### Physical Countermeasures & Detection
- **CISA UAS Guidance**: Detection Technology Guidance for Critical Infrastructure (August 2025)
- **MITRE Counter-Autonomy**: Positioned as economic and security advantage for U.S.
- **AI Physical Security**: 22% adoption rate for threat detection and prediction
- **Vision System Defense**: Comprehensive defense mechanisms required against adversarial threats
- **Security Taxonomy**: Developed for securing autonomous systems against physical and cyber threats
- **Counter-UAS Distinction**: Separated from pure detection technology

#### Environmental & Operational Hazards
- **Deep Learning Recognition**: Methods for automatic environmental hazard recognition in ecosystems (2025)
- **Drone-Wildlife Impact**: Species-specific sensitivities to drone disturbance identified
- **Climate-Robotics Roadmap**: High-impact opportunities for climate-autonomy intersection
- **Wildlife Responses**: Include vigilance, flight responses, physiological stress
- **Environmental Factors**: Altitude, speed, noise, visual stimuli significantly influence performance

#### Regulatory & Legal Constraints
- **UN LAWS Resolution**: Lethal Autonomous Weapons Systems resolution (Dec 2024) - 166 votes in favor
- **Human Rights Impact**: Autonomous weapons identified as hazard to human rights (HRW, April 2025)
- **Two-Tier Approach**: Prohibit some LAWS, regulate others under international law
- **EU AI Act**: Excludes military and security applications from scope
- **No Global Regulation**: Patchwork of partial legal frameworks across jurisdictions
- **Meaningful Human Control**: Requirement becoming central to international treaty discussions
